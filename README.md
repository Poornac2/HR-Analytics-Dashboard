# 👨‍💼 HR Analytics Dashboard (Microsoft Excel)

## 📌 Project Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition trends
- Identify factors influencing employee turnover
- Compare attrition across departments
- Evaluate employee demographics
- Build an interactive HR dashboard
- Support HR decision-making with data insights

---

# 📊 Dataset Overview

The dataset includes employee information such as:

- Employee ID
- Age
- Gender
- Department
- Education
- Job Role
- Salary
- Marital Status
- Business Travel
- Overtime
- Years at Company
- Attrition

**Total Records Analyzed:** 1470 Employees

---

# 🔍 Analytical Approach

## 1️⃣ Data Cleaning & Preparation

- Removed duplicate records
- Handled missing values
- Standardized text fields
- Verified data consistency
- Created Pivot Tables for analysis

---

## 2️⃣ KPI Development

Designed KPI cards for:

- 👥 Total Employees
- 🚪 Attrition Count
- 📉 Attrition Rate
- 💰 Average Salary
- 📅 Average Age
- ⏳ Average Years at Company

---

## 3️⃣ Dimensional Analysis

Performed analysis across:

- Department
- Job Role
- Gender
- Education
- Age Group
- Salary Slab
- Overtime
- Business Travel

Interactive slicers enable dynamic dashboard exploration.

---

## 4️⃣ Trend Analysis

- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Age Group
- Attrition by Salary Slab
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Gender

---

# 📈 Key Findings & Business Insights

## 1. Departments with Highest Attrition

Insight:
Departments showing high attrition require targeted retention strategies.

---

## 2. Overtime Employees Leave More Frequently

Insight:
Employees working overtime have a higher likelihood of leaving.

---

## 3. Young Employees Show Higher Attrition

Insight:
Early-career employees require better engagement and career growth opportunities.

---

## 4. Salary Influences Attrition

Insight:
Lower salary groups experience comparatively higher attrition.

---

## 5. Certain Job Roles Experience Higher Turnover

Insight:
HR should focus retention programs on critical job roles.

---

## 6. Business Travel Impacts Attrition

Insight:
Frequent travel may contribute to employee dissatisfaction.
